✍️ LETTER TO US IN 5 YEARS ✍️

A Time Capsule of Love
INSTRUCTIONS
Each person writes in their own notebook. Take 30-45 minutes. Be honest, be hopeful, be specific.
This letter is a time capsule of who you are today and who you hope to become together.
SECTION 1: What Today Was Like

Capture the present moment. Pick what resonates.

  • Where are we right now? Describe the Arboretum—what do you see, hear, smell?
  • What time of day is it? What's the weather like?
  • How are you feeling in this exact moment as you write this?
  • What did we do today that made you smile or laugh?
  • What was the best conversation we had today?
  • Describe one specific moment from today you never want to forget
SECTION 2: Who We Are Today

Capture a snapshot of your relationship as it exists in January 2026.

  • How would you describe our relationship in January 2026?
  • What are we really good at as a couple right now?
  • What are we still learning or struggling with?
  • What's one thing you love about me today that you hope never changes?
  • What's one inside joke or habit we have right now?
  • If you had to summarize "us" in one sentence today, what would it be?
SECTION 3: What I Hope You Become

Write about your hopes and dreams for your partner's individual growth.

  • What skill or quality do I hope you develop by 2031?
  • What dream of yours do I hope has come true?
  • What challenge do I hope you've overcome?
  • What do I hope you're no longer worried or stressed about?
  • How do I hope you've grown as a person?
  • What do I hope you're proud of yourself for?
SECTION 4: What I Hope We Become

Envision your shared future together as a couple.

  • Where do I hope we're living in 2031?
  • What adventures do I hope we've had together?
  • What traditions do I hope we've built?
  • How do I hope we've deepened our relationship?
  • What do I hope we've learned about each other?
  • What kind of team do I hope we've become?
SECTION 5: What I'm Working On

Make personal commitments about how you'll show up in the relationship.

  • What's one thing I'm committing to work on in myself for our relationship?
  • What's one promise I'm making to you about how I'll show up?
  • What's one way I want to love you better over the next 5 years?
  • What's one fear I'm going to face or habit I'm going to change?
After Writing: Exchange and Keep
How This Works:
♥ After both of you finish writing your letters, exchange them

♥ You keep her letter, she keeps yours

♥ Read them whenever you need encouragement or perspective

♥ Each person becomes the "keeper" of the other's hopes and dreams

♥ This creates a beautiful bond—you're literally holding each other's future in your hands
Writing Tips
Write in pen (it feels more permanent)
Don't edit yourself too much—raw is better than polished
Be specific (not "I hope we travel more" but "I hope we've been to Japan")
Include fears and worries, not just positive hopes
Write like you're talking to a friend, not writing an essay
If you cry while writing, that's good—it means you're being real
Date and sign your letter
Consider adding small drawings or doodles in the margins
FINAL THOUGHT
This letter is a gift to your future selves. Write from the heart. Be vulnerable. Be hopeful.
The act of writing itself creates clarity about what matters most to you both.

Happy writing! ❤️
